Micromolecules Pictures Worksheet Help


In this assignment, you will identify whether a molecule is a Carbohydrate, Protein, Lipid, or Nucleic Acid. There are some fairly easy ways to do this.

\

Carbohydrates

- Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen at a 1:2:1 Ratio. This means that for every 1 Carbon or Oxygen atoms there are, there are 2 Hydrogen.
- Usually in ring format, but not always. An example of ring format is shown below


Proteins

- Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen are all in the molecule somewhere
- Nitrogen exists somewhere in the molecule

Lipids

- Lipids have very little Oxygen atoms

Nucliec Acids

- Carbon, hydrogen, oxygen and nitrogen
- Difference between Nucliec Acids and Proteins is that Nucliec Acids contain a Phosphate group


Answers

C for Carbohydrate, P for Protein, L for Lipid, N for Nucliec Acid
